Wizio

About Us

Founded in January of 2015, Wizio utilizes cutting-edge technology to offer an automated realtor service that connects quality tenants to landlords and property managers faster and cheaper than ever before. The software as a service provides virtual reality apartment showings, automated lease generation and submission, and tenant screening. Wizio aims to enhance the process for everyone by bringing affordable automation to the apartment rental market.

Wizio was co-founded by Cameron Billings and Devon Grodkiewicz. Shortly thereafter, Wizio entered the 2015 Husky Startup Challenge, during which the team expanded, joined by Christopher Canal. Cameron and Chris comprise the core of Wizio’s development team with Cameron acting as CTO.

College-aged tenant’s primary pain point is paying the realtor’s fee, which typically amounts to a month’s rent. College students are forced to lower their rent budgets in order to accommodate for the exorbitant realtor's fees. Wizio provides an affordable alternative to the current expensive and archaic realtor process. On January 1st, 2015, Wizio will launch its online E-realtor service.

The Boston market is comprised of roughly 85,000 apartments, 70-80% of which are leased by realtors, leading to an approximate market potential of $30 million for Wizio. In the U.S., there are more than 20 million landlords and 100 million tenants. Catering to these landlords and tenants are 1.5 million realtors, leading to an annual apartment revenue of $165 billion.

Mission

Wizio was born from the seed of an idea: freedom of information. As our service transitioned from one iteration to the next, our mission became more clear, but more importantly, the plight of the housing industry and the institutions that uphold and maintain its status quo became more apparent to us. Wizio’s mission is to bring honest, transparent information to the apartment market in boston by utilizing crowd sourced information and automation as an E-realtor service.
